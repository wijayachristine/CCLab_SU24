Financioflu Nyctobion

Scientific Name:

Financioflu Nyctobion

Discovered by / at:

Christine Wijaya when interning at the New York Stock Exchange and was told to clean the basement

Family:

Belongs to the family Datafluxidae, characterized by its digital origin and dependency on data and financial transactions for sustenance. Members of this family are highly adaptive, capable of subtly influencing their digital ecosystems while remaining largely invisible within the data streams they inhabit.

Habitat:

Within the network of computers in the basement of the New York Stock Exchange building. However, they learned how to go around the NYSE building.

Appearance:

Resembles the offices in the NYSE trading floor. They have layers of changing color due to how they came to be.

Key Characteristics:

  • Eats some transactions.
  • Colorful.
  • Grows when they eat.
  • No food = dies.
  • Affect the stocks.
  • Can live digitally and physically ONLY IF food is present.

What we know so far about Financioflu Nyctobion? It is a dynamic and influential entity found during the 2008 financial crisis that resides in the NYSE. It feeds on financial transactions thus affecting market behaviors. This creature has a shimmering body that constantly shift colors refelcting the data it is consuming. It thrives in high-activity zones and sleeps during low transaction times. The creature's discovery changes the way we understand the stock market and the finance industry. Scientists speculate there are more of its' family residing in our digital space in different industries that has been affecting the digital space since it was first created.
Guidelines for human interaction with this creature? It is tough for humans to interact with the creature due to the nature of what it needs. We can sometimes see them exploring the New York Stock Exchange building, especially the trading floor, but not for a long time as they have to go back to their digital space to feed. If they do not feed, they will lose their shimmering shifting colors and crystalize into a black obsidian. But we have to watch out for mutations in the future.
